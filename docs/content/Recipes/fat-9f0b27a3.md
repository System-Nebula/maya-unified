Garlic Butter Herb Rice
	
– 220g (8oz) Uncooked Basmati Rice
	
– 20g (1oz) Light Butter
	
– 30g (1.1oz) Garlic, finely chopped
	
– 1.5 Tbsp Fresh Parsley, chopped
	
– Pinch of Salt & Chilli Flakes
INSTRUCTIONS
	
z S T E P 1 
Trim any excess fat from the chicken thighs, then 
place them in a large mixing bowl. Season with 
salt, black pepper, and garlic powder, then add 
a drizzle of honey, sriracha, fresh orange juice, 
orange zest (add extra orange pulp for more texture 
when cooking), and light soy sauce. Mix thoroughly 
until fully combined and the colour changes. Set 
aside to marinate for at least 30 minutes.
	
z S T E P 2 
Slice all the vegetables for the crunchy cucumber 
salad. In a large bowl or dish, add thinly sliced 
cucumbers, red onion, grated carrots, chopped 
red bell pepper, green onion/scallion, and sesame 
seeds, followed by a drizzle of honey and rice 
vinegar. Mix thoroughly until well combined, then 
store in separate containers and refrigerate to keep 
fresh and crisp for the week.
	
z S T E P 3 
Bring a pot of salted water to a boil. Add the 
uncooked basmati rice (rinsed and drained 
beforehand) and cook for 8-10 minutes, until tender 
and fluffy. Drain any excess water and set aside.»»
	» Continue
At the same time, line a sheet pan with parchment 
paper and spread the marinated chicken evenly in 
a single layer. Sprinkle a little extra garlic powder 
and black pepper over the top, then add additional 
orange juice and orange zest. This will help create 
a sticky, caramelized glaze as it cooks. Air fry or 
oven bake for 25-27 minutes at 200C / 400F, until 
golden brown and sizzling.
	
z S te p 4
While the chicken cooks, heat a large pan over 
medium–low heat and melt light butter. Add plenty 
of chopped garlic and cook for about 4 minutes, 
until fragrant and infused into the butter. Add the 
leftover cooked rice, mix well, and allow it to heat 
through. Let the rice cool slightly before adding 
freshly chopped parsley, a pinch of salt, and chilli 
flakes. Mix until fluffy. 
	
z S te p 5
Once the chicken is cooked, slice it into juicy cubes, 
mixing it with the resting juices in the sheet pan for 
extra flavour. Serve and portion with the fluffy garlic 
rice and crunchy cucumber salad, then ENJOY!
IMPORTANT COOKING NOTES
	
z
Aim for at least 30 minutes of marinating, but longer (up to a few hours in the fridge) will deepen the sweet 
spicy citrus flavour and help keep the chicken juicy and tender.
	
z
Spread evenly on a lined sheet pan, squeeze extra orange juice and zest. This will caramelize while cooking 
and create a sticky glaze. To achieve a good crust, you can broil for 2-3 mins at the end
	
z
When making the garlic butter rice, keep the heat medium low and cook the garlic gently. If it browns too 
much, it will turn bitter and overpower the dish.
	
z
Keep the salad in separate meal prep containers and combine right before eating to maintain crunch. The 
vinegar base helps preserve the texture for 2-3 days
	
z
Let the rice cool slightly before folding in the herbs to keep them vibrant and fresh. You could also top each 
container with a sprinkle of fresh herbs right before serving to brighten it up
	
z
When you slice the chicken and mix it back through the juices on the sheet pan, you’re essentially making a 
quick pan sauce. Don’t skip this step - it adds moisture and boosts that orange honey sriracha flavour.
