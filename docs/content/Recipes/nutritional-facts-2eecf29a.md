INGREDIENTS (6-8 SERVINGS)
Chicken Marinade
	
– 1000g (35oz / 2.2lbs) Raw Boneless 
Skinless Chicken Thighs, cubed 
	
– 1.5 Tsp Salt 
	
– 2 Tsp Black Pepper 
	
– 2.5 Tsp Garlic Powder 
	
– 2.5 Tsp Onion Powder 
	
– 2 Tsp Smoked Paprika 
	
– 2 Tsp Ginger Powder 
	
– 3 Tsp Olive Oil (15ml)
Low Calorie Bang Bang Sauce
	
– 100g (3.5oz / ~5 Tbsp) Light Mayo 
	
– 60g (2.1oz / ~4 Tbsp) Fat Free Yogurt 
	
– 50g (1.8oz / 3 Tbsp) Reduced 
Sugar Sweet Chilli Sauce 
	
– 50g (1.8oz / 3 Tbsp) Sriracha 
	
– 20g (1oz / 1 Tbsp) Rice Vinegar 
	
– 1 Tbsp Fresh Parsley, chopped
INSTRUCTIONS
	
z S T E P 1 
Slice the boneless, skinless chicken thighs into 
bite-sized cubes. Place them in a large bowl and 
season with salt, black pepper, garlic powder, 
onion powder, smoked paprika, ginger powder, 
and olive oil. Mix thoroughly until the chicken is 
evenly coated and the colour deepens. Set aside to 
marinate.
	
z S T E P 2 
To make the bang bang sauce, combine light mayo, 
yogurt, sweet chilli sauce, sriracha, rice vinegar, 
and freshly chopped parsley in a bowl. Mix until 
smooth. Separate a small amount of this sauce 
into another bowl - this will be used to baste the 
chicken during cooking.
	
z S T E P 3
Spread the marinated chicken cubes in a single layer 
on a lined sheet pan. Use a brush to lightly coat each 
piece with the reserved bang bang sauce. Bake or air 
fry at 200°C / 400°F for 22-25 minutes until golden 
and slightly crispy. Once cooked, while the chicken is 
still hot, brush over a final layer of bang bang sauce 
to create a glossy, flavour-packed glaze.
	
z S T E P 4 
Allow the chicken to cool slightly, then store the 
chicken whole as cubes in airtight containers, 
making sure to include any tray juices for extra 
flavour and moisture. Keep a portion of the bang 
bang sauce stored separately in a small bottle 
or airtight container in the fridge. This is ideal for 
adding extra sauce when serving or when reheating 
the chicken throughout the week for maximum 
flavour and moisture retention, ENJOY!
BEST WAYS TO SERVE AND PAIR THIS PROTEIN 
This Bang Bang Chicken works extremely well in simple, easy meal prep combinations, for example:
	
–with jasmine or coconut rice and cucumber 
	
–with crispy roasted potatoes and a side salad 
	
–in a tortilla wrap with lettuce and sweetcorn 
	
–mango or pineapple salsa for sweetness and freshness
SAUCE PAIRING SUGGESTIONS 
This chicken also pairs well with:
	
–extra bang bang sauce when serving 
	
–chilli-lime mayo 
	
–yogurt and lemon mixed together
