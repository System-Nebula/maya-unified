How to make
1.	 In a blender, combine garlic, ginger, shallots, green chilies, fresh coriander, salt, ground cumin, lime 
juice, and fish sauce. Blend into a smooth paste, adding a little water if needed to help it come together.
2.	 Cut the chicken into bite-sized cubes and mix with about 2 tablespoons of the green paste until well 
coated. Let it marinate for 10–15 minutes while you prepare the rest.
3.	 Heat a tablespoon of olive oil in a pan over medium heat. Add the marinated chicken and cook for about 
5 minutes until lightly browned and just cooked through. Remove from the pan and set aside.
4.	 In the same pan, add the remaining green paste and cook gently for 1–2 minutes until fragrant. Pour in 
300–400ml of light coconut milk and stir well, letting it simmer for about 5 minutes until the sauce slightly 
thickens.
5.	 Return the cooked chicken to the pan along with sliced green onions and chili pepper. Simmer for another 
5 minutes, then serve hot over jasmine rice or any rice of your choice.
