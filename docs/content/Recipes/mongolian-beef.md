Mongolian Beef
One of my all time favorite takeout meal made Healthy & Macro Friendly! This 
was honestly super easy to make and tastes absolutely amazing!
Macros per serving
573 CALORIES
65g PROTEIN
48g  CARBS
11g FAT
Ingredients (2 servings)
Beef:
	
z
400g (14.1oz) Raw Weight Lean 
Beef Steak Cut Into Thin Strips 
Tip: Cut Diagonally Against the 
Grain of the Meat
	
z
1 Tsp Olive Oil
	
z
1 Tbsp Soy Sauce
	
z
1 Tbsp Rice Vinegar
	
z
1/2 Tsp Baking Soda (This 
is Very Important to Make 
the Beef Really Tender!)
	
z
1 Tbsp Cornstarch
	
z
1 Tsp White Pepper 
or Black Pepper
Sauce:
	
z
2 Tbsp Light Soy Sauce
	
z
1 Tbsp Oyster Sauce 
(You Can Replace with 
Low Sugar Ketchup)
	
z
1 Tsp Rice Vinegar
	
z
1/2 Cup Water
	
z
1 Tbsp Cornstarch
For the cooking process:
	
z
1 Tsp Olive Oil or 
Cooking Spray
	
z
4 Garlic Cloves, Chopped
	
z
1 Tbsp Ginger, Chopped
	
z
1 Red Chilli
	
z
Green Onion/Scallion, 
Cut Into Long Pieces 
	
z
Serve with 120-140g 
(5oz) Cooked Jasmine 
Rice per Serving
How to make
1.	 Slice the steak into thin strips, ideally cutting on a diagonal for tenderness. Marinate the beef with olive oil, soy 
sauce, rice vinegar, baking soda, cornstarch, and white pepper. Mix well and let it sit while you prepare the 
other ingredients.
2.	 Chop your vegetables, then prepare the stir-fry sauce in a bowl by mixing soy sauce, oyster sauce, rice 
vinegar, water, and cornstarch until smooth.
3.	 Heat a pan on high and spray with oil. Add the marinated beef in batches, cooking 1–2 minutes per side until 
browned. Avoid overcrowding the pan. Remove and set aside.
4.	 In the same pan, add a small amount of olive oil, followed by garlic, ginger, red chilli, and green onion. Stir-
fry for 2–3 minutes until fragrant, then pour in the prepared sauce. Let it cook for another minute or so until it 
thickens.
5.	 Return the beef to the pan, toss everything together until well coated, and serve with hot jasmine rice. Garnish 
with sesame seeds and extra green onion, then enjoy!
