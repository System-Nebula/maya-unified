Chipotle Steak
	
– 800g (28oz) Raw Topside/Top 
Sirloin Beef 1 Inch Thick Steaks
	
– 2 Tsp Garlic Powder
	
– 2 Tsp Parsley
	
– 2 Tsp Paprika
	
– 2 Tsp Cumin
	
– 2 Tsp Salt
	
– 2 Tsp Black Pepper
	
– 3 Tsp Olive Oil
Creamy Avocado Sauce
	
– 140g (5oz) Avocado
	
– 200g (7oz) Fat Free Yogurt
	
– Big Handful Coriander/Cilantro
	
– 1/2 Tsp each: Garlic Powder, Salt, Black Pepper
Pico de Gallo
	
– 200g (7oz) Tomato, chopped
	
– 150g (5.3oz) Red Onion, chopped »»
	» Continue
	
– 100g (3.5oz) Red Bell Pepper, chopped
	
– 100g (3.5oz) Green Bell Pepper, chopped
	
– 2 Tbsp Coriander/Cilantro, chopped
	
– 1/2 Tsp each: Salt, Black Pepper
	
– Juice of 1/2 or 1 Lime
Coriander Lime Rice
	
– 210g (7.4oz) Uncooked Basmati 
Rice (or rice of choice)
	
– 1 Tbsp Coriander/Cilantro, chopped
	
– Juice of 1/2 Lime
	
– Salt to taste
Extra Sides
	
– Shredded Lettuce
	
– Black Beans
	
– Sweetcorn
	
– Shredded Mozzarella or Cheddar Cheese
INSTRUCTIONS
	
z S T E P 1 
Season both sides of the steaks evenly with garlic 
powder, parsley, paprika, cumin, salt, pepper, and 
olive oil. Rub the spices in until evenly coated and 
the color deepens then set aside.
	
z S T E P 2 
Make the avocado sauce by blending avocado, 
yogurt, fresh coriander, garlic powder, salt, and 
black pepper until smooth and creamy. Transfer to a 
squeezy bottle for easy storage.
	
z S T E P 3
For the coriander lime rice, bring water to a boil in 
a pot, add the raw basmati rice, then simmer and 
cook for 8–10 minutes until fully cooked and fluffy.
	
z S T E P 4
Once cooked, stir in a generous handful of 
chopped coriander, fresh lime juice, and salt to 
taste. Gently mix until well combined and fluffed up, 
then set aside.
	
z S T E P 5
Heat a pan over medium heat,  add a little olive 
oil or butter and cook the marinated steaks for 4 
minutes per side, adjusting based on thickness. They 
should come out seared and golden brown. Let them 
rest for 4–5 minutes before slicing into juicy cubes.
	
z S T E P 6
Make the pico salsa by combining chopped 
tomato, red onion, red and green bell peppers, 
freshly chopped coriander, salt, pepper, and 
lime juice. Mix until well incorporated. Store this 
separately for easier reheating.
	
z S T E P 7
Make the pico salsa by combining chopped 
tomato, red onion, red and green bell peppers, 
freshly chopped coriander, salt, pepper, and 
lime juice. Mix until well incorporated. Store this 
separately for easier reheating.
IMPORTANT COOKING NOTES
	
z
Marinate the steak for at least 15-20 minutes—overnight is ideal for maximum flavor.
	
z
Cook the steak on medium heat to prevent the spices from burning. For a thick steak, cook for 4 minutes 
per side, then flip and cook for another 2 minutes per side.
	
z
For easy reheating, store the lettuce, salsa, sweetcorn, beans, and avocado sauce separately and add them 
fresh when ready to eat.
	
z
Prepare the salsa and avocado sauce in advance and refrigerate for a few days for convenient future use.
