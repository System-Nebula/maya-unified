Korean Fried Chicken Burgers
You don’t have to give up your favourite foods to lose weight! Korean Fried Chicken Burger is one of the most 
popular fast food item, this healthier version is lower calorie with better macros and more protein!

150g (5.3oz) Red Cabbage, chopped

1 Tbsp Rice Vinegar

z

z
1/2 Tsp Garlic Powder
Sticky Glaze:
	
z
30-40g (1.4oz) Light Soy Sauce
	
z
2 Tbsp Gochujang Paste - 
	
z
20g (1oz) Honey
How to make
1.	 Tenderise the chicken breasts to ensure even thickness, then slice each one 
in half. Season both sides with salt, paprika, garlic powder, and chilli powder.
2.	 Prepare your breading stations: one bowl with an egg wash and another with 
seasoned breadcrumbs. Dip each chicken fillet into the egg wash, then coat 
completely in the crumbs, pressing to ensure full coverage. Place the coated fillets 
on a tray, spray with cooking spray, and bake at 200°C (392°F) for 20 minutes.
3.	 While the chicken bakes, thinly slice the red cabbage, carrots, and cucumber. 
Mix the cabbage and carrots with light mayo and pepper to make a quick slaw. 
In a separate bowl, combine the cucumber with the listed pickling ingredients 
and mix well.
4.	 In a small pot over low heat, add all the ingredients for the glaze. Bring it to a 
gentle bubble and let it simmer for a few minutes until it thickens slightly.
5.	 Once the chicken is cooked and crispy, brush or toss it with the warm glaze 
until evenly coated.
6.	 Toast the brioche buns, then assemble: add a layer of slaw, some pickled 
cucumber, a piece of glazed crispy chicken, a light drizzle of spicy mayo, and 
a sprinkle of sesame seeds. Enjoy!
It’s important to flatten the chicken 
to cook evenly. I place a spatula on 
the chicken and hit it with my hand 
till desired thickness.
When coating the chicken, if it 
doesn’t stick make sure you drip 
excess egg wash.
You can make the slaw and pickled 
cucumbers in batches in advance to 
save time.
When making the glaze, make sure 
it’s on low heat and only boil for a 
minute or two. Colour should be 
slightly darker.
