INGREDIENTS (4 SERVINGS)
Philly Cheesesteak Beef
	
– 800g (28oz) 95% Raw Ground/Mince Beef 
	
– 20g (0.7oz) Grass Fed Butter (or 
any butter for cooking)
	
– 20g (1.5 Tbsp) Minced Garlic
	
– 100g (3.5oz) White Onion, chopped
	
– 75g (2.7oz) Red Bell Peppers, chopped
	
– 75g (2.7oz) Green Bell Peppers, chopped
	
– 75g (2.7oz) Yellow Bell Peppers, chopped
	
– 1.5 Tsp each Salt, Pepper, Paprika, Chilli Flakes
Mac N Cheese Sauce
	
– 140g (5oz) 50% Fat Cream Cheese
	
– 50g (1.8oz) Shredded Mozzarella
	
– 3 to 4 (19g each) Light Cheese Slices
	
– 125g (½ Cup or 4.5oz) Pasta Water 
(after the pasta is cooked)
	
– Garnish Fresh Parsley, chopped
Pasta
	
– 240g (8.5oz) Uncooked Macaroni Pasta
INSTRUCTIONS
	
z S T E P 1
Bring a large pot of water to a boil. Add the 
macaroni and cook for 7–8 minutes until slightly 
underdone. Before draining, reserve 1 cup of pasta 
water (keep extra on hand if needed). Drain the 
pasta and set it aside. 
	
z S T E P 2 
While the pasta cooks, heat a pan over medium 
heat. Melt the butter, then add the minced garlic, 
chopped onion, and mixed bell peppers. Sauté for 
5–6 minutes until the vegetables become soft and 
fragrant.
	
z S T E P 3
Push the vegetables to the edges of the pan to 
create a central well. Add the minced beef along 
with salt, pepper, paprika, and chilli flakes. Break 
the beef into small pieces with a spatula 
	» Continue
and cook on high heat for 6–8 minutes until it is 
fully browned. Once browned, mix the beef with the 
vegetables.
	
z S T E P 4
Reduce the heat to low and add the cream cheese, 
shredded mozzarella, cheese slices, and the 
reserved pasta water. Stir continuously until the 
cheese melts and the sauce becomes smooth, 
creamy, and well combined with the beef and 
vegetables.
	
z S T E P 5
Lastly add the cooked macaroni into the sauce, 
mixing thoroughly to ensure everything is well 
coated. If needed, add extra pasta water to adjust 
the consistency. Finally, distribute the dish evenly 
into 4 servings, garnish with parsley, and ENJOY!
IMPORTANT COOKING NOTES
