INGREDIENTS (5 SERVINGS)
Chicken Marinade
	
– 1000g (35oz) Raw Boneless Skinless 
Chicken Thighs, cubed
	
– 1.5 Tsp Salt
	
– 2 Tsp Black Pepper
	
– 2.5 Tsp Garlic Powder
	
– 2.5 Tsp Onion Powder
	
– 2 Tsp Smoked Paprika
	
– 2 Tsp Ginger Powder
	
– 3 Tsp Olive Oil
Low Calorie Bang Bang Sauce
	
– 100g (3.5oz) Light Mayo
	
– 60g (2.1oz) Fat Free Yogurt
	
– 50g (1.8oz) Reduced Sugar Sweet
Chilli Sauce »»
	» Continue
	
– 50g (1.8oz) Sriracha
	
– 20g (1oz) Rice Vinegar
	
– 1 Tbsp Fresh Parsley, chopped
Lemon Garlic Herb Coconut Rice
	
– 260g (9.1oz) Uncooked White Rice 
(freshly cooked or leftover cooked)
	
– 20g (1oz) Reduced Fat Butter
	
– 35g (1.2oz) Garlic Cloves, finely chopped
	
– 170g (6oz) White Onion, finely chopped
	
– Extra Seasoning: 1 Tsp each Salt, 
Oregano, Parsley, Chilli Flakes
	
– 35g (1.2oz) Shredded/Desiccated Coconut
	
– 2 Tsp Lemon Zest
	
– 1/2 Lemon Juice
INSTRUCTIONS
	
z S T E P 1 
Slice the boneless, skinless chicken thighs into bite-
sized cubes. Place them in a large bowl and season 
with salt, black pepper, garlic powder, onion powder, 
smoked paprika, ginger powder, and olive oil. Mix 
thoroughly until the chicken is evenly coated and the 
colour deepens. Set aside to marinate.
	
z S T E P 2 
To make the bang bang sauce, combine light mayo, 
yogurt, sweet chilli sauce, sriracha, rice vinegar, and 
freshly chopped parsley in a bowl. Mix until smooth. 
Separate a small amount of this sauce into another 
bowl—this will be used to baste the chicken during 
cooking.
	
z S T E P 3 
Spread the marinated chicken cubes in a single layer 
on a lined sheet pan. Use a brush to lightly coat each 
piece with the reserved bang bang sauce. Bake or air 
fry at 200°C (400°F) for 22–25 minutes until golden 
and slightly crispy. Once cooked, while the chicken is 
still hot, brush over a final layer of bang bang sauce 
to create a glossy, flavour-packed glaze.
	
z S T E P4
In a large pan over medium heat, melt a knob of butter. 
Add chopped garlic and onion, and sauté for 5 minutes 
until soft and fragrant. Add salt, oregano, parsley, chilli 
flakes, and shredded coconut. Stir well and cook for 
another 3 minutes to toast the coconut slightly. Add 
your cooked rice, mix thoroughly, then finish with 
lemon zest and a squeeze of lemon juice. Stir until the 
rice is fluffy and aromatic.
	
z S T E P 5
Serve and divide the bang bang chicken and fragrant 
coconut rice evenly into 5 portions. Your high-
protein meals for the week are ready to ENJOY!
