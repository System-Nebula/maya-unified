How to make
1.	 Slice the potatoes into thin, even fries. Place them in a large bowl and season with garlic powder, oregano, 
black pepper, grated parmesan cheese, and cornflour. Lightly spray with cooking spray and toss 
everything together until the fries are evenly coated.
2.	 Preheat your oven to 220°C (425°F). Spread the fries out in a single layer on a lined baking tray, ensuring 
they’re not overlapping. Bake for 25–30 minutes, flipping halfway through, until crispy and golden. 
Alternatively, cook in an air fryer at 220°C for 12–14 minutes, shaking halfway.
3.	 Once the fries are cooked, immediately sprinkle with salt, a bit more parmesan, and some freshly chopped 
parsley for extra flavour and a pop of colour.
4.	 For the dipping sauce, combine light mayonnaise, fat-free yogurt, crushed or finely minced garlic, 
paprika, and a pinch of salt in a small bowl. Stir until smooth. Serve the fries hot with the sauce on the 
side—enjoy!
