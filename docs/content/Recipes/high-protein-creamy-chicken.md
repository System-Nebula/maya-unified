High Protein Creamy Chicken
Easily one of the Top 3 Chicken recipes I’ve ever made! 
This literally tastes unreal and it is SO EASY to make!
Macros per serving
507 CALORIES
 62.5g PROTEIN
48.5g  CARBS
7g FAT
Ingredients
Chicken:
	
z
300g (10.6oz) chicken breasts 
cut into cubes (raw weight)
	
z
1 Tsp Olive oil
	
z
1 Tbsp Paprika or Smoked Paprika
	
z
1 Tsp Garlic powder or fresh Garlic
	
z
1 Tbsp Dried Basil
	
z
1/2 tsp Chilli flakes
	
z
1 Tsp Salt
For the sauce:
	
z
120ml (4.2oz) Low Fat/
Skimmed Milk
	
z
90g (3.1oz) Low/Zero 
Fat Cream Cheese
	
z
15g (0.5oz) grated Parmesan
	
z
1 Tsp Garlic powder
	
z
1 Tsp Basil
	
z
1/4 Tsp Chilli flakes
For cooking:
	
z
Cooking spray
	
z
340g (12oz) Spinach
	
z
Extra seasoning if needed
	
z
Serve with: 140g (5oz) cooked 
white rice per serving or 120g 
(4.2oz) of cooked pasta
How to make
1.	 Cut the chicken breast into small cubes and place them in a bowl. 
Marinate with olive oil, garlic powder, paprika, basil, chilli flakes, and 
salt. Mix thoroughly until the chicken is evenly coated. Set aside.
2.	 In a blender cup, combine milk, cream cheese, parmesan, and the 
seasoning mix. Blend for a couple of minutes until completely smooth 
and creamy.
3.	 Heat a non-stick pan over medium-high heat and spray lightly with oil. 
Add the marinated chicken and cook for about 4 minutes on one side 
until golden. Flip the chicken, lower the heat, then pour the blended 
sauce over it. Gently mix to coat.
4.	 Add a handful of spinach to the pan, then cover and let it simmer for 
8–10 minutes. About halfway through, lift the lid and stir the wilted 
spinach into the sauce.
5.	 Check that the sauce has thickened to your liking. Taste and adjust 
seasoning if needed before turning off the heat.
6.	 Serve the creamy chicken over a bed of white rice. Garnish with fresh 
herbs and enjoy!
When cooking the sauce, If it’s too 
thick add extra milk, if it’s too thin 
then add extra parmesan or cream 
cheese.
Make sure you don’t over cook the 
sauce for too long and keep the 
heat on LOW! Check for the right 
consistency.
Calories and macros will vary based 
on ingredients and brands you use!
