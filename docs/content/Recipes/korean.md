– 25g (1oz / 1.5 Tbsp) Minced Garlic 
	
– 40g (1.4oz / 1.5 Tbsp) Gochujang Paste
Spicy Korean Sauce
	
– 150g (5.3oz / 7 Tbsp) Fat Free Yogurt 
	
– 80g (2.8oz / 4.5 Tbsp) 50% Fat Mayo 
	
– 35g (1.2oz / 2 Tbsp) Gochujang 
	
– 1 Tsp Garlic Powder 
	
– 4 Tsp Rice Vinegar
INSTRUCTIONS
	
z S T E P 1 : M A K E T H E KO R E A N M A R I N A D E
In a mixing bowl, combine light soy sauce, dark soy 
sauce, honey, minced garlic, and gochujang. Mix 
until smooth and fully combined. This marinade will 
be used for both the beef and optional rice.
	
z S T E P 2 : C O O K T H E B E E F
Heat a pan over medium-high heat. Add a small 
amount of olive oil if needed, then cook the 1000g 
minced beef for about 6-8 minutes, breaking it 
apart with a spatula until fully browned with no pink 
remaining.
	
z S T E P 3 : A D D T H E M A R I N A D E & S TO R E
Lower the heat to medium, then add half of the 
prepared Korean sauce, the chopped green onions, 
and sesame seeds. Stir well and cook for 4-5 
minutes until the beef becomes glossy, golden, and 
slightly caramelised. Once done, remove from heat 
and transfer the cooked beef into airtight glass 
containers, spreading it evenly so it cools quickly 
before storing for meal prep.
BEST WAYS TO SERVE AND PAIR THIS PROTEIN 
Perfect for simple, Korean-inspired meal prep bowls. Great options include:
	
–with steamed jasmine rice and cucumbers 
	
–with kimchi and stir-fried veggies 
	
–in lettuce cups with rice and sliced green onion
SAUCE PAIRING SUGGESTIONS 
This Korean beef goes well with:
	
–Spicy Korean Sauce (included in this recipe) 
	
–gochujang yogurt drizzle (yogurt, gochujang, lime) 
	
–honey-soy garlic sauce (honey, soy, garlic)
