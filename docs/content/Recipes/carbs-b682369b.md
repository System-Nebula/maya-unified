CARBS
16g 
FAT
Two comfort food classics that taste absolutely indulgent 
and fit your goals
TAP FOR VIDEO
THE MEAL PREP COOK BOOK 
77
INGREDIENTS (5 SERVINGS)
Korean BBQ Chicken
	
– 1000g (35oz) Raw Boneless Skinless 
Chicken Thighs, fat trimmed
	
– 1.5 Tsp Salt
	
– 2 Tsp Black Pepper
	
– 2 Tsp Garlic Powder
	
– 2 Tsp Onion Powder
	
– 1.5 Tsp Red Pepper Flakes or Chilli Flakes
	
– 2-3 Tsp Olive Oil
	
– 25g (1oz) Light Butter (for cooking)
After Cooking Glaze
	
– 20g (1oz) Minced Garlic
	
– 40g (1.4oz) Gochujang Hot Sauce (brand: O’Food)
	
– 30g (1.1oz) Honey
	
– 20g (1oz) Rice Vinegar
Creamy Mac & Cheese
	
– 230g (8oz) Uncooked Macaroni Pasta
	
– 300ml Skimmed Milk
	
– 180g (6.3oz) Light Cream Cheese
	
– 3/4 Tsp Salt
	
– 3/4 Tsp Black Pepper
	
– 1 Tsp Paprika
	
– 3/4 Tsp Nutmeg
	
– 120g (4.2ox) Low Fat Cheddar Cheese
Korean Cucumber Salad
	
– 300g (10.6oz) Cucumber, thinly sliced
	
– 150g (5.3oz) Red Onion, thinly sliced
	
– 100g (3.5oz) Green Onion, chopped
	
– 3/4 Tsp Red Pepper Flakes
	
– 1 Tsp Garlic Powder
INSTRUCTIONS
	
z S T E P 1
Trim excess fat from the boneless chicken thighs 
and place them in a large mixing bowl. Season with 
salt, black pepper, garlic powder, onion powder, red 
pepper flakes, and olive oil. Mix until evenly coated, 
then set aside to marinate.
	
z S T E P 2
Bring a pot of water to a boil. Add macaroni or your 
preferred pasta and cook for 8-10 minutes until al 
dente. Drain and set aside.
	
z S T E P 3
In a mixing bowl, combine thinly sliced cucumbers, 
red onion, green onion, red pepper flakes, garlic 
powder, sesame seeds, gochujang sauce, light soy
sauce, and rice vinegar. Mix thoroughly, then store 
in a separate container and refrigerate.
	
z S T E P 4
Heat a pan over medium-high heat and melt a little 
butter. Cook the marinated chicken thighs for 
4-5 minutes on each side until golden and crispy. 
Remove from the pan and set aside.
	
z S T E P 5
In the same pan over low heat, add minced garlic, 
gochujang hot sauce, a drizzle of honey, and rice 
vinegar. Stir to form a rich, vibrant glaze. Add the 
chicken back to the pan and coat evenly until glossy 
and golden. Remove from heat and set aside.
	
z S T E P 6
In the same pan over medium-low heat, add milk, 
cream cheese, salt, black pepper, paprika, and a 
pinch of nutmeg. Stir until smooth, then add low-
fat cheddar and mix until melted. Add the cooked 
macaroni and stir until fully coated and creamy.
	
z S T E P 7
Slice the glazed Korean chicken into bite-sized 
cubes. Distribute evenly across 5 servings along 
with the mac & cheese and cucumber gochujang 
salad. Your high-protein meals are ready to ENJOY!
