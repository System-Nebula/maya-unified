INGREDIENTS (5 SERVINGS)
Garlic Steak Bites 
	
– 1000g (35.2oz) Raw Sirloin or Topside 
Beef, cut into 1 inch cubes
	
– 2 Tsp Salt
	
– 2 Tsp Black Pepper
	
– 3 Tsp Italian Herb Seasoning
	
– 2 Tsp Garlic Granules
	
– 2 Tsp Parsley
	
– 4 Tsp Olive Oil to marinate
	
– Avocado Oil Cooking Spray
Fluffy Mashed Potatoes
	
– 1000g (35.2oz) Raw Potatoes, 
peeled cut into chunks
	
– 25g (1oz) Light Butter
	
– 50g (1.7oz) Light Evaporated Milk, warmed
	
– Pinch of Salt to taste
Creamy Mushroom Sauce
	
– 200g (7.1oz) Brown or Chestnut Mushroom
	
– 3-4 Sprigs of Fresh Thyme or Dried Thyme
	
– Pinch Salt & Pepper
	
– 1.5 Tbsp Dijon Mustard
	
– 80g (6.3oz) Light Cream Cheese
	
– 250g (8.8oz) Light Evaporated Milk
Roasted Garlic Parm Broccoli
	
– 200g (7.1oz) Broccoli or Broccolini, chopped
	
– 100g (3.5oz) Red Onion, chopped
	
– 80g (2.8oz) Grated Parmesan
	
– 1 Tsp Garlic Granule
	
– 1.5 Tsp Italian Herbs
	
– 1 Tsp Salt
	
– 1 Tsp Chilli Flakes
	
– Avocado Oil Cooking Spray
INSTRUCTIONS
	
z S T E P 1 
Slice the steaks into 1-inch cubes and place them 
in a mixing bowl. Season generously with salt, black 
pepper, Italian herbs, garlic powder, parsley, and a 
drizzle of olive oil. Mix until evenly coated, then set 
aside to marinate briefly.
	
z S T E P 2 
Wash and peel the potatoes, then slice them into 
thick chunks. Place them in a pot of cold water and 
bring to a boil. Cook for about 20 minutes, or until 
fork-tender. Drain the water, then cover the pot 
with a lid and let the potatoes steam for 5 minutes.
	
z S T E P 3 
Once steamed, use a potato masher to mash the 
potatoes gently until smooth and free of clumps. While 
still steaming hot, add a knob of butter, warm milk, and 
a good pinch of salt. Use a spatula to mix thoroughly 
until perfectly smooth and fluffy, then set aside.
	
z S T E P 4
Line a sheet pan with chopped broccoli florets, 
red onion, grated Parmesan, Italian herbs, garlic 
powder, salt, and chilli flakes, then spray with »»
	» Continue
avocado cooking oil. Mix well with your hands until 
fully coated and spread into an even layer. Oven 
roast or air fry for 18 minutes at 180°C / 370°F, until 
golden and crispy.
	
z S T E P 5
Heat a pan over medium high heat and spray with 
cooking oil. Cook the steak bites for 3-4 minutes 
per side, until seared and golden. Set aside and 
repeat with the remaining steak.
	
z S T E P 6
In the same pan over medium heat, add the sliced 
brown mushrooms. Cook for about 5 minutes, until 
softened. Reduce the heat, then add fresh thyme, 
salt, black pepper, Dijon mustard, light cream 
cheese, and evaporated milk. Stir continuously with 
a spatula until melted, smooth, and richly creamy.
	
z S T E P 7
Serve the steak bites alongside the mashed 
potatoes and Parmesan roasted broccoli, then top
with the creamy mushroom sauce and ENJOY!
IMPORTANT COOKING NOTES
	
z
Start the potatoes in cold water. This ensures they cook evenly from the inside out and prevents a gluey texture.
	
z
Always steam the potatoes after draining (5 minutes covered). This removes excess moisture, helping the 
mash become light and fluffy instead of watery.
	
z
Mash the potatoes while hot, before they cool. Adding butter and warm milk at this stage allows them to 
absorb properly for a smooth, creamy consistency.
	
z
Avoid over-mashing. Overworking potatoes breaks down the starch and can make them gummy; stop once 
smooth and lump-free.
	
z
Pat the steak dry before searing for the best crust. Moisture prevents browning and causes the steak to steam.
	
z
Cook steak bites in batches. Overcrowding the pan drops the temperature and stops proper searing.
	
z
Lower the heat before adding mustard, cream cheese, and evaporated milk. High heat can cause the sauce 
to split or become grainy.
