INGREDIENTS (4 SERVINGS)
Beef
	
– 800g (28oz) 95% Raw Ground/Minced Beef
	
– 1.5 Tbsp Sesame Seeds
	
– 50g (1.8oz) Green Onion, chopped
Korean Marinade
	
– 60g (2.1oz) Light Soy Sauce
	
– 40g (1.4oz) Dark Soy Sauce
	
– 25g (1oz) Honey
	
– 20g (1.5 Tbsp) Minced Garlic
	
– 30g (1.1oz) Gochujang Paste
For The Fried Rice
	
– 2 Tsp Olive Oil »»
	» Continue
	
– 100g (3.5oz) White Onion, finely chopped
	
– 150g (5.3oz) Carrot, grated
	
– 3 Whole Eggs 
	
– 200g (7oz) Uncooked White Rice or 
530g Leftover Cooked Rice
	
– 50g (1.8oz) Green Onion, chopped
Spicy Korean Sauce
	
– 50g (1.8oz) 50% Fat Mayo
	
– 20g (1oz) Gochujang
	
– 1/2 Tsp Garlic Powder
	
– 2 Tsp Rice Vinegar
INSTRUCTIONS
	
z S T E P 1 
In a mixing bowl, combine light & dark soy sauce, 
honey, minced garlic and gochujang. Mix until 
smooth and well combined. You will use this sauce 
for the both the beef and rice.
	
z S T E P 2 
Heat a pan over medium-high heat, add olive oil, 
then cook the minced beef for 6 minutes until fully 
browned with no pink bits remaining. Use a spatula 
to break up the meat while it cooks.
	
z S T E P 3
Lower the heat to medium, add half of the prepared 
Korean sauce along with chopped green onions 
and sesame seeds. Stir well and cook for another 
4–5 minutes until the beef turns golden and crispy 
then set aside.
	
z S T E P 4
In the same pan over medium heat, add chopped 
onions and grated carrots. Cook for 5 minutes until 
softened, then push them to one side of the pan and 
add the whisked eggs.
	
z S T E P 5
Continuously stir the eggs until scrambled, then mix 
them with the vegetables until well incorporated.
	
z S T E P 6
Add your leftover cooked rice to the pan along with 
the remaining Korean sauce and more chopped 
green onions. Stir thoroughly until every grain of rice 
is evenly coated.
	
z S T E P 7
Return the cooked crispy beef to the pan and 
gently mix until fully incorporated. Garnish with 
sesame seeds.
	
z S T E P 8
For the Korean spicy sauce, mix together light mayo, 
gochujang, garlic powder, and rice vinegar until 
smooth. Serve the beef fried rice evenly into 4 
portions with a drizzle of spicy Korean sauce and 
ENJOY!
IMPORTANT COOKING NOTES
	
z
Use half of the sauce for the beef and the other half for the fried rice. If needed, add extra soy sauce or mix 
in a little water to increase volume.
	
z
When cooking the beef, let most of the moisture evaporate before adding the sauce. Allow it to cook for a 
few minutes until golden brown and crispy.
	
z
Swap whole eggs for egg whites if preferred. Cook on low heat until scrambled for a softer texture.
	
z
After adding the sauce to the rice, mix thoroughly and cook for 3–4 minutes before adding the beef to 
ensure even flavor distribution.
