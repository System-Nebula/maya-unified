Why I Use Grams (g) as My Main Form of Measurement
When it comes to meal prepping and cooking with precision, I prefer to use grams (g) as the 
standard unit of measurement. Why? The reason is simple: grams give you a much more accurate 
and consistent way to measure ingredients. Unlike cups or tablespoons, which can vary in volume 
depending on how ingredients are packed or even the brand you use, grams are always the same.
For example, 100 grams of chicken breast will always weigh the same, no matter the size of the 
breast or the way it's cut. This consistency is key when you're trying to hit specific macro or calorie 
goals, especially for fitness-focused meal plans. Using grams ensures that you're tracking your food 
intake more accurately, which is vital for those working on weight loss, muscle gain, or any other 
specific nutritional goals.
Additionally, grams help with portion control, ensuring you don’t overestimate or underestimate 
the amounts you’re consuming. It also simplifies scaling recipes, so you can easily adjust them 
depending on how many servings you need.

–
