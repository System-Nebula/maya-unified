Why Batch Cooking Proteins
Works So Well (The Science Behind It)
Batch cooking isn’t just convenient - there are real food science reasons why pre cooked, 
marinated proteins taste better, stay juicier, and reheat beautifully.
1.	 Marinades get stronger over time
Salt, acids (lemon, vinegar), yogurt, and spices break down muscle fibres gently, allowing the 
seasoning to:
	
–penetrate deeper
	
–distribute evenly
	
–improve tenderness
Your protein often tastes better on day 2 or 3 than immediately after cooking.
2.	Resting and chilling improve juiciness
After cooking, proteins release moisture. As they cool:
	
–fibres relax
	
–juices redistribute
	
–structure stabilises
This makes pre cooked proteins less likely to dry out when reheated.
3.	Proper storage protects texture
Airtight storage slows moisture loss, limits oxidation, and prevents rubbery surfaces. This 
keeps your protein tender, juicy, and flavourful the entire week.
4.	Reheating works better with pre stabilised proteins
Once chilled, protein reheats more gently and evenly. This leads to:
	
–minimal juice loss
	
–soft, tender texture
	
–the ability to re-crisp 
in an air fryer or pan
This is why well-stored meal prep chicken often tastes better than poorly stored leftovers
5.	Flavour compounds continue to develop
Spices like paprika, cumin, coriander, chilli, garlic, and herbs naturally deepen over time, giving your protein:
	
–richer flavour
	
–greater complexity
	
–a more satisfying 
taste each day
