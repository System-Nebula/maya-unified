INSTRUCTIONS
	
z S T E P 1 
Slice your beef into 1-inch thick steaks, then 
season both sides generously with garlic powder, 
chili powder, salt, black pepper, and a drizzle 
of olive oil. Rub the spices in thoroughly until the 
steaks are fully coated. Set aside to marinate while 
you prep the rest.
	
z S T E P 2 
Bring a pot of water to a boil and add penne pasta. 
Cook for 7–8 minutes until al dente. Before draining, 
reserve about ½ cup of pasta water to help adjust 
the sauce later. Drain and set the pasta aside.
	
z S T E P 3 
In a blender cup, add milk, cream cheese, grated 
parmesan, garlic powder, oregano, salt, chili 
powder, and some of the reserved pasta water. 
Blend until smooth and creamy. Set aside—this will 
be the base of your sauce.
	
z S T E P 4
Get a large pan on medium-high heat, add a bit of 
olive oil or butter, then cook the marinated steaks 
for 3–4 minutes on each side until a nice golden-

Once cooked, set the steaks aside to rest for 5 
minutes—this helps retain the juices when you slice 
them later.
	
z S T E P 5
In the same pan on medium heat, add sliced onions 
and bell peppers. Cook for about 5 minutes until 
soft and fragrant, scraping up any leftover steak bits 
for added flavor.
	
z S T E P 6
Lower the heat slightly to medium-low and pour in 
the creamy sauce from the blender. Let it simmer 
for 3–4 minutes, stirring gently, until it starts to 
thicken. Then add your cooked penne pasta and 
toss everything together until each piece of pasta is 
well coated and creamy.

Finally, slice the rested steaks into thin strips. Serve 
the creamy fajita pasta evenly into 4 portions, top 
each serving with sliced steak, and garnish with fresh 
parsley. Your high-protein meal is ready to ENJOY!
