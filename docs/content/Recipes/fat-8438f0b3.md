INSTRUCTIONS
	
z S T E P 1 
Slice your beef into thin strips. Place in a mixing 
bowl and marinate with light and dark soy sauce, 
black pepper, chili flakes, chili powder, garlic paste, 
ginger paste, sesame seeds, and olive oil. Mix 
thoroughly until all pieces are well coated, then set 
aside to marinate while you prep other ingredients.
	
z S T E P 2 
Heat a pan over medium-high heat, add a small 
amount of light butter, and sear the marinated 
beef for 2–3 minutes per side until golden brown 
and slightly crispy. Do not overcrowd the pan—work 
in batches to ensure the beef sears properly. Once 
cooked, set the beef aside.
	
z S T E P 3
In the same pan on medium heat, add a bit more 
butter if needed, then sauté chopped garlic, 
gochujang paste, chopped green onion (white 
parts), and grated carrots. Stir-fry for 2–3 
minutes »»
	» Continue
until fragrant and the vegetables have softened 
slightly.
	
z S T E P 4
Add your cooked jasmine rice to the pan and 
stir well, ensuring each grain gets coated in the 
gochujang mixture. Cook for 2–3 minutes, mixing 
until the rice turns a vibrant orange color and is 
heated through.
	
z S T E P 5
To the rice, add kimchi juice, chopped kimchi, 
additional green onion (green parts), and a splash 
of soy sauce. Mix thoroughly till well incorporated, and 
cook for another 2–3 minutes so the flavors meld.
	
z S T E P 6
Finally, add the cooked beef back into the pan. 
Gently mix everything together. Let it heat through 
for another 1–2 minutes. Garnish with extra sesame 
seeds, divide into 4 servings, and ENJOY!
