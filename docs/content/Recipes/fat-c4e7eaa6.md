Butter Garlic Steak Bites
	
– 900g (32oz) Raw Topside/Top 
Sirloin Beef, 1-inch cubed
	
– 2 Tsp Salt
	
– 2 Tsp Black Pepper
	
– 1.5 Tsp Parsley
	
– 2 Tsp Oregano
	
– 2 Tsp Garlic Powder
	
– 1.5 Tsp Smoked Paprika
	
– 2 Tsp Olive Oil
Cooking & Chimichurri Glaze
	
– 30g (1.1oz) Reduced Fat Butter

– 2 Tbsp Fresh Parsley, chopped
	
– 1 Tsp Chilli Flakes
	
– Juice of ½ Lemon
	
– Flaky Salt to Garnish 
Cheesy Mashed Potatoes
	
– 850g (31oz) Raw White Potatoes, 
peeled 2-inch chunks
	
– 150g (5.3oz) Light Evaporated Milk
	
– 100g (3.5oz) Shredded Mozzarella Cheese
	
– 1 Tsp Garlic Powder
	
– 1 Tsp Salt
	
– 1 Tsp Black Pepper
INSTRUCTIONS
	
z S T E P 1 
Slice the beef steaks into cubes. In a bowl, season 
with salt, black pepper, parsley, oregano, garlic 
powder, smoked paprika, and olive oil. Mix until 
evenly coated.
	
z S T E P 2 
Peel the potatoes and cut them into thick chunks. 
Place them in a pot of water and boil for 12–14 
minutes until fork-tender. Drain then cover with a 
lid for 5 minutes to allow the remaining steam to 
evaporate any excess moisture.
	
z S T E P 3 
Using a potato masher, mash the potatoes 
until smooth. Stir in evaporated milk, shredded 
mozzarella, garlic powder, salt, and pepper until 
the mash is creamy and cheesy.
	
z S T E P 4
Heat a pan over medium-high heat and add a 
little light butter. Sear the steak bites for about 
2 minutes per side until they develop a golden, 
charred crust.
	
z S T E P 5
Lower the heat to low, then add extra butter, 
minced garlic, plenty of parsley, chilli flakes, and 
a squeeze of lemon juice. Stir until the beef is 
thoroughly coated in this chimichurri flavoured 
butter.
	
z S T E P 6 
Serve the juicy steak bites alongside the cheesy 
mashed potatoes. Divide evenly into 4 containers 
for your high-protein meal prep, and ENJOY!
IMPORTANT COOKING NOTES
	
z
Season steak bites generously; topside beef is a great lean option.
	
z
Peel and cut potatoes into evenly sized chunks for even cooking. Boil for 12–14 minutes until fork-tender, 
then drain and let steam before mashing.
	
z
After adding cheese and milk, mix well until the mash is smooth and the cheese is fully melted.
	
z
Sear steak bites over medium-high heat for 2 minutes per side until a golden-brown crust forms—avoid 
overcrowding. Lower the heat before adding the chimichurri mix, stirring until fully coated.
	
z
For reheating, warm everything in a pot on low heat for 5 minutes; add a splash of milk if the mash is dry.
