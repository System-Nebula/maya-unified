INGREDIENTS (4 SERVINGS)
Fajita Chicken
	
– 700g (25oz) Raw Chicken Breasts, 
butterflied halves
	
– 1 Tbsp Oregano
	
– 1 Tbsp Garlic Powder
	
– 2 Tsp Cumin
	
– 1 Tbsp Smoked Paprika
	
– 2 Tsp Salt
	
– 2 Tsp Black Pepper
	
– 2-3 Tsp Olive Oil
Fajita Rice
	
– 230g (8.1oz) Uncooked Basmati Rice/
Approx. 520g Cooked Rice
	
– 400-440g (14oz-15.5oz) Water »»
	» Continued
	
– 1 Tsp each: Salt, Paprika, Cumin, 
Garlic Powder, Turmeric
	
– 75g (2.7oz) Red Onion, thinly sliced
	
– 75g (2.7oz) White Onion, thinly sliced
	
– 75g (2.7oz) Red Bell Pepper, thinly sliced
	
– 75g (2.7oz) Green Bell Pepper, thinly sliced
Green Sauce
	
– 200g (7oz) 0% Greek Yogurt
	
– 60g (2.1oz) 50% Fat Mayonnaise
	
– 4 Whole Garlic Cloves
	
– Big Handful Coriander/Cilantro
	
– ½ Tsp each Salt & Pepper
	
– Juice of ¼ Lemon
INSTRUCTIONS
	
z S T E P 1 
Butterfly slice the chicken breasts into thinner 
fillets/cutlets. Season both sides evenly with 
oregano, garlic powder, cumin, smoked paprika, 
salt, black pepper, and olive oil. Rub the spices into 
the chicken until fully coated, then set aside.
	
z S T E P 2 
Thinly slice the onions and bell peppers for later. 
For the creamy green sauce, blend yogurt, mayo, 
garlic cloves, fresh coriander, salt, black pepper, 
and lemon juice until smooth. Adjust seasoning to 
taste and set aside.
	
z S T E P 3 
Heat a pan over medium heat and add olive oil. 
Cook the chicken cutlets for 3–4 minutes per side 
until golden brown and fully cooked. Set aside and 
repeat until all the chicken is done.
	
z S T E P 4
In the same pan over medium heat, add the sliced 
onions and bell peppers. Sauté for 4–5 minutes 
until soft and fragrant. Stir in the washed uncooked 
rice along with the spice mix (salt, paprika, cumin, 
garlic powder, turmeric).
	
z S T E P 5
Mix everything well and toast the rice for 2 minutes, 
then add water or stock. Bring to a gentle simmer, 
cover, and cook on low heat for 8-10 minutes until 
the rice is fluffy and fully cooked.
	
z S T E P 6
Slice the chicken cutlets into thin strips, then 
serve over the fajita-seasoned rice. Drizzle 
generously with the creamy green sauce and 
ENJOY!
IMPORTANT COOKING NOTES
	
z
Season the chicken thoroughly on both sides to ensure even flavor in every bite.
	
z
Toast the rice with the veggies and spices for a deeper, more aromatic flavor before adding water.
	
z
Let the rice come to a gentle boil, then cover and simmer for 8–10 minutes until fully cooked and fluffy.
