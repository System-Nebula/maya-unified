How to make
1.	 Cut the chicken breast into cubes, then season with salt, pepper, paprika, and garlic powder. For a crispy 
coating, add cornflour and a pinch of baking powder. Mix thoroughly until all the chicken pieces are evenly coated.
2.	 Heat a pan over high heat and spray with cooking spray or add a bit of olive oil. Cook the chicken for 3–4 minutes on 
each side until golden and crispy. Once done, transfer to a plate lined with paper towel to absorb excess oil.
3.	 In the same pan, lower the heat and add chopped garlic, ginger, and chilli flakes. Sauté for about 1 minute until 
fragrant. Add orange juice, soy sauce, honey, and orange zest to the pan. Stir well, bring to a boil, then reduce the 
sauce for 3–4 minutes to concentrate the flavor.
4.	 In a small bowl, mix cornflour (or cornstarch) with a bit of water, then pour it into the pan. Stir continuously until 
the sauce thickens. Add the crispy chicken back into the pan and toss to coat it well in the sticky orange sauce.
5.	 Garnish with sesame seeds and chopped green onion. Serve hot with rice and enjoy!
