Garlic Parmesan Steak Bites

For Cooking & Final Coating

Alfredo Pasta
	
– 80g (3oz) White Onion, chopped
	
– 15g (1 Tbsp) Garlic Cloved, chopped
	
– 1 Tsp each: Salt, Black Pepper, Italian

Cream Cheese

– 210g (7.4oz) Uncooked Linguine

– 50g (¼ Cup) Pasta Water 
(Optional for sauce consistency)
INSTRUCTIONS
	
z S T E P 1
Slice the beef steaks into bite-sized cubes. Season 
with salt, black pepper, Italian herbs, parsley, 
garlic powder, smoked paprika, and olive oil. Mix 
well until evenly coated.
	
z S T E P 2
Bring a pot of water to a boil, then add your pasta 
of choice. Cook for 8–10 minutes until al dente. 
Reserve 1/4 cup pasta water, then drain the rest and 
set aside.
	
z S T E P 3 
Heat a pan over medium-high heat, add butter, and 
sear the steak bites for 2 minutes per side until a 
golden-brown crust forms.
	
z S T E P 4
Once cooked, lower the heat and add extra 
butter, minced garlic, and grated parmesan. 
Stir until the »»

steak bites are well coated in this flavourful butter, 
then set aside. Repeat for the remaining steak bites.
	
z S T E P 5
In the same pan over low heat, sauté chopped 
garlic and onion for 5 minutes until soft and 
fragrant. Stir in the extra seasonings (salt, pepper, 
Italian herbs, parsley, smoked paprika).
	
z S T E P 6
Mix until the spices are well combined, then add 
evaporated milk, cream cheese, parmesan, and 
reserved pasta water. Stir gently until smooth and 
thickened.
	
z S T E P 7

stirring until fully combined and coated. Serve with 
the juicy steak bites, and your high-protein meal
