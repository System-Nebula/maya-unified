INGREDIENTS (5 SERVINGS)
Garlic Steak Bites
	
– 1000g (35oz) Raw Topside/ Top Sirloin Beef, 1 inch 
cubed
	
– 2 Tsp Salt
	
– 2 Tsp Black Pepper
	
– 2 Tsp Garlic Powder
	
– 2 Tsp Parsley
	
– 3 Tsp Olive Oil
	
– 30g (1.1oz) Reduced Fat Butter (for cooking + extra)
	
– 20g (1oz) Minced Garlic
	
– Pinch of Chilli Flakes (omit for no spice)
	
– 1 Tbsp Fresh Parsley, chopped
Creamy Mashed Potatoes
	
– 1100g (39oz) Raw Potatoes, cut into chunks
	
– 20g (1oz) Light Butter
	
– 80g (2.8oz) 50% Fat Cream Cheese »»
	» Continue
	
– 100g (3.5oz) 1% Milk
	
– 1 Tsp Salt
	
– 1 Tsp Black Pepper
	
– 1 Tsp Garlic Powder
Garlicky Alfredo Sauce
	
– 15g (1oz) Reduced Fat Butter
	
– 1 Tsp Smoked Paprika
	
– 1/2 Tsp Salt or to taste
	
– 1/2 Tsp Chilli Flakes (omit for no spice)
	
– 1 Tbsp Dijon Mustard
	
– 20g (1.5 Tbsp) Minced Garlic
	
– 1 Tbsp Fresh parsley
	
– 200g (7oz) Blended Cottage Cheese
	
– 250-250g (7-8.8oz) 1% Milk
INSTRUCTIONS
	
z S T E P 1 
Slice the beef steaks into 1-inch cubes. Season 
generously with salt, black pepper, garlic powder, 
parsley, and olive oil. Mix thoroughly until the steak 
bites are evenly coated, then set aside to marinate 
while you prep the potatoes.
	
z S T E P 2 
Wash, peel, and chop the potatoes into thick, even 
chunks. Place them in a pot of cold water, bring 
to a boil, and cook for 12–14 minutes until just fork-
tender. Drain the water, then cover the pot with a 
lid and let the potatoes steam for 5 minutes— this 
helps soften them further for a smooth mash.
	
z S T E P 3 
Use a potato masher to mash the steamed potatoes 
gently until smooth and fluffy. Avoid over-mashing 
to keep the texture light. Add a knob of butter, 
cream cheese, milk, garlic powder, salt, and black 
pepper. Mix gently until fully combined and creamy.
	
z S T E P 4
Heat a pan over medium-high heat, add butter, 
and sear the marinated steak bites for 2–3 
minutes on each side until golden brown and 
crusted. Lower the heat, then add more butter, 
minced garlic, chilli flakes, and chopped parsley. 
Toss the steak bites in the buttery glaze until 
coated and aromatic, then set aside
	
z S T E P 5
In a blender, combine cottage cheese and milk. 
Blend for 2 minutes until smooth. Back in the same 
pan over low heat, melt a small amount of butter. 
Add smoked paprika, chilli flakes, salt, mustard, 
plenty of minced garlic, and parsley. Sauté for 5 
minutes until fragrant. Pour in the blended cottage 
cheese and stir until the Alfredo sauce becomes 
creamy and slightly thick. Remove from heat.
	
z S T E P 6
To serve, portion the creamy mash and steak bites 
into 5 meal prep containers. Drizzle generously with 
the homemade Alfredo sauce. Your flavour-packed 
highprotein meals are ready to ENJOY!
