INGREDIENTS (4 SERVINGS)
Mexican Chicken
	
– 800g (28oz) Raw Chicken Breast, cubed
	
– 2 Tsp Oregano
	
– 2 Tsp Garlic Powder
	
– 2 Tsp Paprika
	
– 1 Tsp Cumin
	
– 1 Tsp Chilli Powder
	
– 1.5 Tsp Salt
	
– 1 Tsp Black Pepper
	
– Juice of 1 Lime
	
– 2 Tsp Olive Oil (for cooking)
Mexican Rice
	
– 100g (3.5oz) White Onion, finely chopped
	
– 150g (5.3oz) Red Bell Pepper, chopped
	
– 100g (3.5oz) Black Beans
	
– 400g (14oz) Canned Tomatoes (or fresh chopped)
	
– 210g (7.4oz) Uncooked Basmati Rice
	
– 1 Tsp each: Oregano, Garlic Powder, Paprika, 
Cumin, Chilli Powder, Salt, Black Pepper
	
– 310g (11oz) Water (or chicken stock, extra flavour)
	
– 1 Tbsp Fresh Coriander/Cilantro, chopped
INSTRUCTIONS
	
z S T E P 1 
Slice your chicken breasts into bite-sized cubes. 
Season generously with oregano, garlic powder, 
paprika, cumin, chili powder, salt, black pepper, 
and a squeeze of lime juice. Mix thoroughly until 
every piece is well coated - if the color doesn’t 
deepen, add more seasoning.
	
z S T E P 2 
Heat a pan over medium heat and add a drizzle 
of olive oil or cooking spray. Cook the seasoned 
chicken for about 7–8 minutes, turning occasionally 
until golden brown and fully cooked through. Once 
done, remove from the pan and set aside.
	
z S T E P 3
In the same pan, add chopped onions and bell 
peppers. Sauté for 2–3 minutes until slightly soft. 
Then add black beans (drained and rinsed), 
uncooked washed rice, and all the extra seasoning 
for added flavor.
	
z S T E P 4
Toast the rice with the veggies and beans for 1–2 
minutes. Pour in canned chopped tomatoes and 
water (enough to cover the rice by about 1 inch). 
Stir well and bring to a gentle boil. Cover with a lid 
and simmer on low heat for 12–15 minutes until the 
rice is fully cooked and fluffy. Stir halfway through to 
ensure even cooking and prevent sticking.
	
z S T E P 5
Once the rice is cooked, add the cooked chicken 
back in, along with chopped fresh coriander. Mix 
everything thoroughly until well combined. Taste and 
adjust seasoning if needed.
	
z S T E P 6
Divide evenly into 4 portions, garnish with extra 
coriander or lime wedges if desired, and ENJOY!
IMPORTANT COOKING NOTES
	
z
Toast the uncooked rice with spices for 1–2 minutes to enhance flavor before adding liquids.
